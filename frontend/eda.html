05 Univariate Analysis — Salary
Salary distribution — histogram
Distribution shape

The salary distribution is right-skewed — most listings cluster in the ₹8–12L range, which represents entry-to-mid level roles. A smaller high-earning tail (₹12–18L) reflects senior and specialist positions.

⚠️ Data limitation: Adzuna returns role-level salary benchmarks, not individually reported salaries. This explains why only 5 unique salary values exist across ... listings. The shape is directionally correct — absolute values should be cross-referenced with platforms like Glassdoor or AmbitionBox.

Skewness describes the asymmetry of a distribution. A right-skewed (positively skewed) distribution has most values on the left, with a long tail pulling right. In salary data this is extremely common — most people earn moderate salaries, but a small number of high earners pull the average (mean) above the median. Always compare mean vs median for salary data.

07 Correlation — Experience vs Salary
Experience (max years) vs average salary
⟳ Calculating correlation...
What this tells us

The relationship between experience and salary is positive but non-linear. Entry-level roles (0–2 years) cluster around ₹9L. Mid-level (5–7 years) shows a salary jump. Beyond 10 years the data gets sparse and noisy — fewer listings, higher variance.

The most important jump happens between 2→5 years. That's the critical window — moving from junior to mid-level. Target this transition deliberately.

Correlation measures how strongly two variables move together. The Pearson r coefficient ranges from -1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive). r = 0.6–0.8 is considered strong in social/economic data. Correlation ≠ causation — experience doesn't cause higher salary directly, but employers use it as a proxy for skill depth.

10 Key Takeaways — Executive Summary
🐍
Python + SQL are non-negotiable They appear in the highest proportion of listings across every role type — from Data Analyst to ML Engineer. These are the baseline, not differentiators.
🏙️
Bangalore leads in volume, not always salary Most jobs are in Bangalore but smaller cities like Indore and Jaipur sometimes show higher averages — smaller market, less competition.
⚙️
Data Engineers earn the most At ₹13L average vs ₹8.6L for Business Analysts — engineering skills (Spark, Kafka, Airflow) command a clear premium in the Indian market.
📈
The 2→5 year window is the salary inflection point Experience correlates positively with salary, but the biggest jump happens in mid-level transition. Focus on depth over breadth early in your career.
☁️
Cloud is no longer optional AWS, Azure and GCP appear across analyst, engineer and scientist roles. Adding even one cloud certification meaningfully expands your eligibility.
⚠️
Data limitation to note Salary figures are Adzuna role-level benchmarks. date_posted is partially approximated for older listings. Conclusions are directionally accurate — treat absolute numbers as indicative, not precise.
